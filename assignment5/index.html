Animal Farm

George Orwell

Animal Farm was published in 1945. This book is a satrical allegory of the Russian Revolution which began in 1917 and led the way for Joseph Stalin to take power.

The animals get together in the barn to listen to Old Major, one of the main characters. He is a boar, and speaks about his dream. He says that the Man is the oppressor of all the animals, and the animals would be a better life if they rebelled and ran from the farm. He explained they need to advoid all humans and then teaches the animals a song called Beasts of England.

This book has several themes throughout. some themes include desire for power, class systems, language and propaganda and more.
